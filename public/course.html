Module 02 / 06
READING SIGNALS
Not every signal is worth trading. This module teaches you exactly which signals to act on and which to ignore completely.
🤖
How the 4-Pass Engine WorksPriceAction AI runs 4 separate AI analyses on every chart: Pass 1 reads chart structure, Pass 2 reads Smart Money Concepts, Pass 3 finds the best entry, Pass 4 confirms and sharpens everything. The final signal is the result of all 4 agreeing.

Signal Grades — Your Most Important Filter

GRADE SYSTEM
🥇
A+ Grade — TRADE THIS4+ confluences perfectly aligned. Strong trend, key level, clean entry, excellent R:R. These are the setups institutional traders wait for.
✅
A Grade — TRADE THIS3 strong confluences, clear trend, decent R:R above 1:2. Solid setups worth taking with normal position size.
⚠️
B Grade — SKIP or PAPER TRADE2 confluences, identifiable direction but setup isn't clean. If you take these, use half your normal size. Better to wait.
❌
C and D Grade — NEVER TRADEWeak or marginal setup. The AI is showing you the analysis but this is NOT a trade. Ignore it completely.

Other Filters to Check

📊
Confidence Score
Only trade signals with 70%+ confidence. Below 70% means the AI isn't sure enough. Combined with grade — you want A/A+ AND 70%+ confidence.
⚡
Risk/Reward
Check the R:R label. Good or Excellent only. If TP1 R:R is below 1:2, skip the trade no matter how good everything else looks.
🎯
Market Bias Match
The signal verdict must match the market bias. Don't take a BUY signal if the market bias says Strongly Bearish. They must align.
⏳
When You Get WAIT
WAIT means no good setup right now. Try a different asset, come back in 1-2 hours, or wait for the next session. Never force a trade.

ENTERING TRADES
You have a valid A+ or A signal. Now what? This module covers exactly how to enter, where to set your stop, and how much to risk.
🛑
Never Chase EntriesThe AI gives you an entry trigger — a specific price or price action that confirms the trade. Wait for that trigger. Don't just market order in because you're excited. Chasing entries ruins your R:R and puts you in at a worse price.
1
Wait for the entry trigger
The AI specifies an entry trigger — this might be "close above resistance," "retest of order block," or "break of consolidation." Wait for exactly this to happen before entering. If the trigger never fires, the trade never happens. That's fine.
2
Calculate your position size
This is the most important step. Risk maximum 1% of your account per trade — no exceptions.

Formula: Account size × 0.01 ÷ stop loss distance in pips/points = position size

Example: $1000 account, 50 pip stop → risk $10 → position size accordingly. On Friday or in uncertain conditions, risk 0.5%.
3
Set your stop loss immediately
The AI gives you an exact stop loss price with a structural reason. Set it the moment you enter — not after. Place it exactly where the AI says, not closer (you'll get stopped out by noise) and not further (you'll risk more than 1%).
4
Set your take profit levels
Set TP1 and TP2 as limit orders immediately. Don't manage it manually while watching — emotions will make you exit too early or too late.

TP1 = take 50% off your position automatically
TP2 = close remaining 50%

Module 05 / 06
MANAGING TRADES
Most traders know how to enter. Few know how to manage. This is where consistent profits come from.
THE TRADE MANAGEMENT RULES
✅
When TP1 hits → move SL to breakevenThe moment price reaches TP1, move your stop loss to your entry price. The trade is now risk-free. You've locked in profit and can't lose.
✅
Close 50% at TP1Take half your position off at TP1. Lock in real profit. Let the other half run to TP2 with a free trade.
❌
Never move SL further awayIf the trade goes against you, your stop loss exits you. Never move it further to avoid being stopped out. That turns a small loss into a big one.
❌
Never add to a losing tradeIf price is going against you, do not add more. Ever. Your analysis was wrong — adding more just makes the loss bigger.
✅
Exit if no movement in 4-6 hoursIf price hasn't moved in the expected direction within 4-6 hours, exit the trade. The setup has failed. Cut it and look for a new opportunity.
✅
Log every trade in the journalEvery single trade — win or loss — goes in the journal. This data is more valuable than any signal. After 20 trades it will show you exactly what's working.

The Journal — Your Most Valuable Tool

📓
What to track
After every trade: mark Win or Loss, enter the actual R:R achieved. After 20-30 trades, the journal shows your win rate by grade — this tells you which signals actually work for YOU.
📈
What to look for
If A+ signals are hitting 65%+ win rate → only trade A+. If a specific session shows better results → focus there. The data customizes the system to YOUR trading.
💡
The Math of 1:2 R:R at 50% Win RateYou only need to win 50% of trades with a 1:2 R:R to be profitable. Every win pays $2, every loss costs $1. At 50% you net $0.50 per trade. At 65% win rate you net $1.30 per trade. You don't need to be right most of the time — just make sure wins are bigger than losses.

Module 06 / 06
THE WINNING MINDSET
The final and most important module. Everything else means nothing if you can't control your emotions and follow your rules.
🧠
The Hard Truth80% of traders lose money. Not because they don't know technical analysis. Because they can't follow their own rules when money is on the line. Emotions — fear and greed — make them deviate from their system. This module is about preventing that.
1
Only trade money you can afford to lose
The moment you're trading scared money — money you need for rent, food, or bills — your decision making breaks down completely. Fear makes you close winners too early and hold losers too long. Start with an amount where losing it all wouldn't change your life.
2
Wait 30 minutes after a loss before analyzing again
Revenge trading is the fastest way to blow an account. After a loss, your brain wants to immediately "get the money back." This leads to taking bad setups, over-sizing, and breaking every rule. Wait at least 30 minutes. Go for a walk. The market will always be there.
3
Stop after 3 losses in a row
Three consecutive losses means one of two things: the market is in a condition the AI doesn't read well today, or you're not executing properly. Either way, stop trading for the day. Come back tomorrow with fresh eyes. Continuing usually leads to a 4th and 5th loss.
4
Paper trade for the first 30 trades
Before risking real money, run the system for 30 trades using paper trading (fake money). Track everything in the journal. See your actual win rate. Prove to yourself the system works before you commit real capital. Most people skip this step and regret it.
5
Think like a sniper, not a machine gun
You might only get 3-5 A+ or A grade signals per week. That feels like not enough — but it's exactly right. One clean A+ trade at 1:3 R:R is worth more than five B grade trades. Quality over quantity. Wait for the perfect shot. Professional traders pass on 70% of setups.
🎯
The Long Game
Compounding 2-4% per week turns $1,000 into $50,000+ in a year. But only if you protect capital. One bad week of revenge trading can wipe months of gains. Small consistent wins beat big risky bets every time.
⭐
Your Edge
The AI gives you an analytical edge. Your discipline turns that edge into profit. Neither one works without the other. A disciplined trader with a simple system beats an undisciplined trader with perfect signals every time.
