Step 2 — Only Trade These Hours (EST)
⭐ Best Scalp Window
9:30 – 10:00am
Eastern Time (EST)
First 30 minutes of NY open. Maximum volume, fastest moves, tightest spreads. This is when scalping works best — price moves fast and predictably off key levels.
🚫 Avoid These Times
11:30am – 2:00pm
Eastern Time (EST)
Midday chop. Price moves randomly with no direction. Scalping during this window is a losing battle — you'll get chopped out repeatedly.
Step 3 — Scalp Profitability Tips
✅ Do This
Only scalp during the first 30 min of NY open (9:30–10am)
Wait for a clear displacement candle before entering
Use tight SL — no more than 5–8 ticks on ES/NQ
Take profit at the nearest liquidity level
Max 3 scalp trades per session — stop after 2 losses
Trade ES (MES) or NQ (MNQ) only — cleanest scalp action
❌ Never Do This
Never scalp during midday (11:30am–2pm) — low volume, choppy
Never move your stop loss after entering
Never hold a scalp trade for more than 15 minutes
Never scalp against the 15m trend
Never revenge trade after a loss — walk away
Never scalp on low-grade signals (C or D) — wait for A or B
⚡ 6 Rules for Scalp Profitability
1
Only trade A or B grade signals — never C or D on a scalp
2
All 3 timeframes (1m + 5m + 15m) must point the same direction
3
Require a displacement candle (strong momentum candle) before entry
4
Minimum 1:1.5 R:R — if TP isn't 1.5x your SL away, skip the trade
5
Stop trading after 2 consecutive losses — protect your account
6
Close all scalp trades before 10:00am if not hitting TP — avoid reversal

Step 3 — When to Enter Swing Trades
⭐ Best Entry Time
End of Day
3:00–4:00pm EST · After NY Close
Enter swing trades at the end of the day after daily candle closes. This gives you a confirmed daily candle to base your analysis on — much more reliable.
✦ Also Good
NY Open
9:30–10:30am EST
NY open often provides the first clean swing entry of the day. Price reacts strongly to overnight levels — good for catching the start of a multi-day move.
Step 4 — Swing Trade Profitability Tips
✅ Do This
Always check Daily chart first — only trade in its direction
Use wider SL — swing trades need room to breathe
Target minimum 1:3 R:R — the wider SL must be justified
Enter at end of day on confirmed daily candle closes
Use smaller position size — you're holding overnight
Let winners run — move SL to breakeven once +1R
❌ Never Do This
Never swing trade against the daily trend
Never use the same position size as day trading
Never hold through a major news event (FOMC, NFP)
Never close early just because it dips — swings need time
Never take a swing trade on a C or D grade signal
Never ignore the 4H structure — it defines your entry zone
🌊 6 Rules for Swing Trade Profitability
1
Daily chart (Slot 1) is law — only trade in the direction of the daily trend
2
Require minimum 1:3 R:R — the wider SL must be compensated by bigger TP
3
Enter on confirmed candle closes — not mid-candle reactions
4
Move SL to breakeven once price hits +1R in your favor — protect the trade
5
Reduce size by 50% vs day trading — overnight risk is real
6
Close or hedge before major news events (FOMC, CPI, NFP)
